Digital Marketing Metrics and KPI's

Get control and near real time monitoring of your digital marketing campaigns by selecting pre-built metrics and KPI's

from Zoom360 Metrics library


Average Lead Score

Measure the Quality of Marketing and Sales Leads. Lead Scoring is the process of measuring the quality of marketing and sales leads based on predetermined criteria and targets.


Avg. Lead Score = Sum of Individual lead scores over a given period of time (day, week, month, etc.) / Total number of days in that period

Average Time on Page

Measure the duration of time a viewer spends on a page.


(Time visitor A spends on page + Time visitor B spends on page + â€¦ + Time visitor N spends on page)/Total visitors on page

Bounce Rate

Measure the number of visitors that arrive at your site and leave without navigating to any other page on your site. Monitoring your bounce rate helps you identify how effective your content and design are at attracting further interest in what your site has to offer.


(Visitors leaving the site after only viewing one page/ Total site visitors)x100

Brand Awareness Metric

Track how people are hearing about your brand on social media and search engines.


Facebook Posts and Fans,Twitter Followers, Brand Mentions,Branded Search,Brand Awareness and comparison time periods,Brand Awareness Benchmarks,Brand Awareness Challenges

Click Through Rate (CTR)

Measure how often your site on an SERP prompts users to click and navigate to your page.


Visitors that click on page result/Visitors that view the page result

Content Downloads

Measure the total number of pieces of content that have been downloaded.


Total number of content downloads

Cost per Action (CPA)

Measure the amount of money put towards driving conversions.


Payments for conversions/Total number of conversion

Cost Per Lead

Measure the cost-effectiveness of marketing campaigns.


f1=(Total cost of campaign / # of leads generated) ,f2=( Cost per Lead = Cost of Generating Leads / Total Leads Acquired)

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

Measure to total amount of capital it costs to gain a new customer.


Total marketing spend on customer acquisition/Total new customers

Customer Attrition

Measure the rate at which your company loses customers over time.


(Customers lost in a given time period/Total number of customers)x100

.

Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)

Measure the amount of gross profit that is generated from a customer over the entire time they do business with a company. With the Customer Lifetime Value metric, you can monitor just how much revenue every new customer will bring into the company throughout their lifetime.


Gross margin (%) x Length of lifetime in pay periods x Revenue per subscription per pay period

Customer Share by Category

Measure the number of customers that come from a specified category, such as Region, Industry, or Company Size.


(Total number of customers that fall within category N/Total number of customers)x100

Dormancy Rate

Measure the number of customers that are not using your product in a given time period, the Dormancy Rate metric gives your team insight into possibly customer loss.


(Customers not using the product within time period/Total number of customers)x100

End Action Rate

Measure the number of customers that come from a specified category, such as Region, Industry, or Company Size.


(Total number of customers that fall within category N/Total number of customers)x100

First Visit Metric

Understand how people are finding your website and how engaged they are on their first visit. New visitors are categorized as either engaged or not engaged depending upoon Average Time on Site and Pages per Visit


Average Time on Site, and Pages per Visit.

Funnel Conversion Rate

Measure the rate of which leads move through the marketing funnel.Monitoring the funnel conversion rate gives your team valuable insight into the efficiency of their marketing efforts and into the volume trends of their funnel.


(Number of leads that have moved to the next stage in the funnel/Leads in funnel stage)x100

Goal Completion Rate

Measure how effective your campaigns are at prompting your audience to complete a goal.Goals: A marketing objective for your audience to complete, such as subscribing to a mailing list. Lead: An individual that has expressed interest in your product or service by completing a goal.


# of visits / # of leads and # of leads per channel / # of wins

Incremental Sales

Demonstrate how your marketing campaigns are resulting in increased sales revenue.


Baseline Sales: What you expect to sell for a given time period without marketing. Lead: An individual that has expressed interest in your product or service by completing a goal. Wins: New customers generated by a marketing campaign. Campaign: A marketing communication on a dedicated channel intended for a business' target audience.
